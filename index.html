About Arrival In Greece

To Start off the trip my mom dropped me off in Chicago. The check in went smoothly; However, the flight did not. My flight got delayed about 4 hours and we sat on the plane for about three of those. I ended up missing my connection in Istanbul. When I arrived in Istanbul, I found my gate almost immediately, but I had about a 3 hour layover. So I went and explored the Airport. It was very nice and I have some pictures of it above. I then boarded the airplane and made it to Greece. I made it through to customs very quick and grabbed my bags to go get picked up. Week 2 and 3

Sorry for the late upload. These last two weeks were very busy and chaotic. Last weekend was very fun. I was able to explore downtown Athens on Friday and find a park to watch a sunset. It was very amazing to see and such an amazing view along with seeings some historical sites as well. Saturday I went to the downtown and did some shopping in the modern square to explore some greek shops and the differences between american stores and the ones here in Greece. Then on Sunday I was able to make a trip to the Cape Sounio which was a place that I had wanted to visit and it was considered one of the most beautiful sunsets in Greece.

The week was then started off with one of the biggest snowstorms in Athens in the last 40 years. We got almost an entire foot of snow or more in one day. It drifted up for several feet. We lost power and the city was not prepared for the snow and the city lost power and was down for several days. Classes were cancelled for a few days. Along with this we couldn't get food for a few days and had to live off of sandwhiches and cereal. During this time, We built an enormous snowman named George. After this week, I spent the weekend exploring a few museusms to learn more about Greek Culture. I visited the Acropolis museum and the Cycladic Museum of Art both were very interesting. On Sunday, a group of us went to the beach and swam even though the water was very very cold. Then we were treated with my third beautiful sunset in two weekends. It was an amazing way to end the weekend.

This weekend was another school trip that was arrange for the study abroad students. Our group took a bus and first stopped at the Corinth Canal. It was very big and the edges were very steep. Santorini

We were able to get a couple days off because of students who had to take exams. This provided us an opportunity to head to Santorini for 5 days. It was a very beautiful island that is an old Volcano. The first day we got there it was raining and we visited a museum. It then cleared up, and we were able to explore the old townn of Fira. It was very beautiful and it was perched on top of a cliff. We walked down some steps to visit the old port of the island. The next day we visited a town at the north end of the Island called Oia. It was so beautiful out that day and very sunny. Sunday we ended up going for coffee and shopping for souveniers because it was very windy before we caught our flight out.

Meteora

This weeked I went to Meteora with a few of my friends. It was an amazing experience. We took a train there through the Greek country side which was very beautiful. We were able to see so many pretty mountains along the way. The visit was amazing and we had a great guide. It was a long day and we were able to see the mountains and visit 3 of the 6 monastaries. On the way back, we had to take a bus because of harsh snow in Athens. The guide seemed to enjoy giving us a little scare before telling us that the travel agency had arranged for a bus to take us back.

Meteora is an amazing site which contains monastaries that were built on the very top of mountains. There were orignally many of them but now only 6 remain. The mountains themselves are very interesting as there was a lake thousands of years ago that got swept away and left the moutains that look like gumdrops. Then, later monks decided to make it a project to live a life of solitude on top of them. The project took very long; it took 20 years to take materials to the top, but took only 22 days to build when the materials were up there. It was a very amazing place to visit because of the history and the beauty.
